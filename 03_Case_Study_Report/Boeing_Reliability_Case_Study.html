1. Executive Summary

Objective: Streamline unscheduled maintenance operations by isolating the root causes of component failures within the Boeing 737 ATA 32 Landing Gear system.

Outcome: Architected a complete data pipeline that processes over 500 unstructured Federal Aviation Administration Service Difficulty Reports. The dashboard uncovered a forty percent seasonal surge in failures during the May-June period and demonstrated that ninety-six percent of logged defects triggered immediate Aircraft on Ground (AOG) groundings. These predictive analytics empower maintenance operations to pivot from reactive firefighting to proactive inventory and resource staging.

2. The Business Challenge

Airlines face massive operational and financial losses when maintenance teams react blindly to component failures. Critical metrics were locked in unstructured regulatory reports, causing:

  • Operational Disruption: 473 of the 491 logged faults led directly to AOG conditions, pulling revenue-generating aircraft out of service.
  • Safety Blind Spots: Lack of engineering insight into component stress during the Climb Phase (retraction risks).
  • Resource Misallocation: Mechanics treating nuisance sensor errors with the same urgency as critical structural failures.

3. Technical Architecture: Medallion Data Pipeline

To ensure zero data loss from messy regulatory files, I engineered a resilient database architecture.

  • Bronze Layer (Staging): Raw datasets were ingested strictly as TEXT to bypass schema crashes caused by blank strings and dirty mechanic logs.
  • Silver Layer (Production): Engineered a dynamic SQL View to cast mathematical types, standardize date formats, and safely handle unknown flight hours as true NULL values, preventing lifecycle tracking corruption.

4. Data Modeling & Advanced Analytics

To enable high-performance multi-dimensional analysis, a strict Star Schema was constructed connecting the Fact Table (Daily Defects) to Dimension Tables (Date, Monthly Temperature). A standard one-to-many relationship was utilized to maintain model efficiency and prevent ambiguity paths.

Severity Risk Index (DAX Logic)

Automated DAX logic classified severity based on text keywords within the mechanic logs to prioritize safety-critical issues over nuisance faults.

Severity Score = SWITCH(TRUE(),
    CONTAINSSTRING([PartCondition], "FIRE") || 
    CONTAINSSTRING([PartCondition], "SHEAR"), 10,  // Critical
    CONTAINSSTRING([PartCondition], "LEAK") || 
    CONTAINSSTRING([PartCondition], "FAIL"), 5,    // Operational
    1                                              // Nuisance
)

  • Predictive Seasonality: Correlated defect rates with ambient temperature, proving a 40% surge in Brake and Tire failures during the May-June peak. Action: Pre-position inventory at high-temp hubs prior to May.
  • Maintenance Strategy Shift: Discovered that 57% of defects were sensor/functional issues rather than structural. Action: Reallocate training budgets toward avionics troubleshooting to reduce repair times.

6. Visual Experience & UX Design

The dashboard was designed to mimic internal aviation software, utilizing the Boeing Brand Palette (#0039A6 Blue, #D62620 Red) for executive clarity.

  • Drill-Through Audit: Users right-click specific failure bars to access a hidden Defect Log to verify raw mechanic notes.
  • Custom Tooltips: Implemented hover-over visuals to reveal operational context (like MEL Deferral Status) without cluttering the main UI.
  • Dual-Axis Correlation: Engineered a Combo Chart to visually prove the correlation between ambient heat spikes and component failure rates.
